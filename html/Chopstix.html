Chopstix

Overview
Chopstix is a mobile application designed to connect foodies who are interested in Asian cuisine and allow them to discover new restaurants wherever they go.

Being Asian-American, it can be a difficult task to find quality Asian cuisine in the States. There are also not many forums specifically for this ordeal. These two issues led me to create an app concept catered towards Asian food in addition to social networking between foodies. I, myself, am a foodie with a food Instagram so I am able to utilize my personal experience and gripes and my fellow foodies' opinions.
Role
UX Designer

Duration
~2 weeks, Sept. 2020

Tools
Procreate, Adobe XD, Photoshop
The Problem \

There are a lot of mobile applications that are revolved around food, and the topic of food has become quite popular in recent years, especially due to Korean mukbangs. However, there is no application dedicated to being a social forum and review app simultaneously. There are also no applications dedicated to strictly finding Asian cuisine.
The Solution \

Chopstix is a mobile application that integrates restaurant recommendations, a community forum, and reviews catered towards Asian cuisine.





The Process \
01. Research \

Utilizing my network, I interviewed 3 foodies on Instagram and asked them various questions, such as what food apps they use, their ideal features in a foodie app, how they discover foods and foodies, and if they have difficulty finding Asian cuisine. I also interviewed someone who is not a typical foodie so I could accomodate to those interested in trying new foods.

I also analyzed each app's reviews, sorting by both critical and most useful to obtain an open perspective of what users were both happy and unhappy about. Utilizing the most common complaints, I targeted these issues.

Competitive Analysis \

I chose top food review apps in the App Store, which were Yelp, Foursquare, and the Infatuation. I also chose ChowBus due to its focus on Asian cuisine. The key features that I compared were recommendations, bookmarks, homepage, and forum.

04. Reflection \

This was the first personal UX project I'd ever completed and I learned a lot from the process! I found it interesting from my interviews and research to see what users value in food apps and apps in general. As for what I would change about what I did, I would do more statistical research, A/B testing, and gather some user feedback. If I have more time, I will do more iterations and testing, as it is always possible to improve and add more features, and may implement a dark mode.
